Lakmal Meegahapola

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at ETH Zurich, advised by Prof. Catherine Jutzeler. I got my PhD in Electrical Engineering from EPFL in February 2024, advised by Prof. Daniel Gatica-Perez, and the thesis won an EPFL EDEE PhD Thesis Distinction Award üåü. My research focus is at the intersection of ubiquitous computing, human-computer interaction, digital health, and responsible AI/ML.

Previously, I was a Student Researcher at Google Research (Mountain View, CA, USA) working with Dr. Venky Ramachandran, Dr. Kai Kohlhoff, and Dr. Vidhya Navalpakkam; a Research Intern at Nokia Bell Labs (Cambridge, UK) working with Prof. Daniele Quercia and Dr. Michael Eggleston; and a Visiting Researcher at the Mobile Systems Group of University of Cambridge, hosted by Prof. Cecilia Mascolo. Before starting my PhD, I worked as a Research Engineer at SMU-CMU Living Analytics Research Centre at SMU Singapore, advised by Prof. Archan Misra. I got my BSc in Computer Science and Engineering with a 1st class from University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka in January 2018.

I am an editorial board member of the IEEE Pervasive Computing journal, and an Associate Chair of the ACM CHI conference. I have also been part of the program committee of ACM ICMI, ACM ISWC, ACII, IEEE Percom, among other venues. I have reviewed over 50 papers for venues/journals such as ACM IMWUT (Ubicomp), ACM HEALTH, ACM Computing Surveys, ACM CHI, and IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing.

For my work, I was üèÜ named one of the top four for Gaetano Borriello Outstanding Student Award at ACM Ubicomp 2023, üèÜ recognized as a Rising Star in Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) in 2024, and üèÜ selected as a Young Researcher at 11th Heidelberg Laureate Forum 2024. Our work has üèÜ won a Distinguished Paper Award at ACM IMWUT 2023, üèÜ the Special Recognition for Sustainable Practices at ACM CHI 2024, üèÜ awarded a President's Award for Scientific Research from the Sri Lankan President, among other awards. My work has also been recognized at prestigious events such as Microsoft Imagine Cup World Finals, Asia Pacific ICT Alliance (APICTA), and National Best Quality ICT Awards (NBQSA, Sri Lanka) and has been covered by local and international press. Further, I was a member of the Sri Lankan International Olympiad team for Astrophysics.

NOTE: If you are a self-motivated master/exchange student at ETH Zurich or EPFL interested in a semester/thesis project on using machine/deep learning for research in areas such as healthcare, biomedical engineering, or mobile/wearable sensing, feel free to drop me an email.
